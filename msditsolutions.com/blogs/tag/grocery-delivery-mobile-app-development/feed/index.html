Monetization or business models for grocery apps:

Before you can begin with Grocery Delivery App Development, you need to choose a perfect business model to earn revenue. It is important to have a great revenue value to sustain for years in this competitive market. Some of the options that you can choose are: 

  • Aggregated

Well, this business model is very generic. Here, the vendors partner with local grocery stores. This model covers all the functionalities of a vendor to place in the platform and works perfectly as a chain model. Everything can be done within a single place. Under this model, you will function as a mediator between the owner and the shopper. The development of such apps will also cost less. 

By exploring the menu list, the users can add the required items to the cart. Once done, they can check out the products and complete the payment. Once the payment is made, the users will get a confirmation notification, and they can track the order in real-time. The delivery of the groceries will be managed by the grocery store owner.

  • Dedicated

You can also prefer to go for a dedicated business model. Here you will control your services. Under this model, the sellers and vendors register on the platform, and they utilize your services to supply groceries to the customers. But you need to provide them with delivery executives. 

  • Single Store App

In this grocery app model, you will develop the app for your store, and you will be responsible for dealing with all the tasks, like maintaining the app, keeping the menu updated, collecting payments, delivering the items, and resolving customers’ queries, and more. 

  • Subscription

Here, the users will buy a membership to use the app for a fixed period to enjoy the benefits of some premium services, like faster and free delivery, extra discounts, and more. 

  • Commission

Well, this may be an old business model, but it works. In general, for every sale through the app, the sellers will get commissions. At the same time, a small percentage of money is charged to the buyers for purchasing the groceries through the app and the sellers who register their portals on your platform. 

No matter what business or app model you choose, the primary purpose of developing a grocery delivery mobile app will be to make the process easier and more comfortable for your users while offering them an amazing user experience.

Understanding the Cost of Grocery Delivery App Development:

Well, there are different factors that can affect the Grocery Delivery Mobile App Development Cost. Some of the common factors to consider here are: 

  • The complexity of the grocery delivery app.
  • App development team size
  • Number of app platforms 
  • Region of app development
  • Integration of third-party apps. 

The complexity of the grocery app will be greatly affected by the design and features that you want to integrate into the app. The cost will be higher if you go for complex features and designs. Then, you need to choose between the platforms, i.e., Android or iOS. If you want to develop an app that can run on both these platforms, the cost will be high. 

The Grocery Delivery Mobile App Development Cost per hour will also vary from one region to another. For example, developers from the USA may charge around USD 50 to USD 300 per hour. In the case of European developers, the cost can range between USD 30 to USD 200, and Indian developers may charge USD 10 to USD 100 per hour.

Have a look at the below-given estimate for your grocery app as well, with simple Android coding and limited features. 

  • UI/UX design- USD 1500 to USD 3000
  • Technical documentation- USD 1000 to USD 2000
  • Front-end and back-end development cost- USD 10,000 to USD 20,000
  • QA and testing- USD 2000 to USD 4000

Going by this general quote, it can be said that the Grocery Delivery Mobile App Development Cost for creating a basic app for a single platform would be around USD 10,000 to USD 35,000. If you want to develop a cross-platform or hybrid app, then the cost can go up from USD 40,000 to USD 50,000. However, with a cross-platform grocery delivery app, you can perform much better in the market.
